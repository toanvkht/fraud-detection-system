What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where criminals impersonate legitimate organizations or individuals to trick you into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

How Phishing Works

Attackers typically use one or more of these tactics:

  • Urgency: Creating a sense of emergency to make you act without thinking
  • Authority: Pretending to be someone in a position of power or trust
  • Fear: Threatening negative consequences if you don't comply
  • Curiosity: Using enticing offers or information to lure you in

Real-World Examples

Learn from actual phishing attempts

Example 1: Fake Bank Alert

High Risk

Subject: URGENT: Suspicious Activity on Your Account

From: security@bank-alerts.com

"Dear Customer,

We have detected unusual activity on your account. Your account will be suspended in 24 hours if you do not verify your identity immediately.

Click here to verify: http://verify-account-now.com

Thank you,
Security Team"

Red Flags Identified:

  • Generic greeting ("Dear Customer")
  • Creates urgency and fear
  • Suspicious sender domain
  • Suspicious URL (not the bank's official website)
  • Requests immediate action

Example 2: Package Delivery Scam

High Risk

Text Message:

"Your package could not be delivered due to incomplete address. Update your info here: bit.ly/pack3456
- FedEx"

Red Flags Identified:

  • Unexpected message about a package
  • Uses URL shortener to hide real destination
  • Creates urgency
  • Not from official FedEx number/domain

Example 3: Fake Job Offer

Medium Risk

Subject: Congratulations! Job Offer

From: hr@company-hiring.biz

"Hello,

Congratulations! After reviewing your resume, we would like to offer you a position with our company. The salary is $5,000/month for remote work.

To process your application, please provide your SSN and bank details for direct deposit setup.

Best regards"

Red Flags Identified:

  • Unsolicited job offer
  • Requests sensitive information (SSN, bank details)
  • Suspicious domain (.biz instead of company domain)
  • Offer seems too good (high pay for easy work)
  • No interview process mentioned

Security Best Practices

1

Verify Before You Trust

Always verify the sender's identity through official channels before responding to any request for information or action.

  • Contact the organization directly using official contact information
  • Don't use contact details provided in the suspicious message
  • Check official websites by typing the URL directly
2

Use Strong Authentication

Protect your accounts with strong, unique passwords and multi-factor authentication (MFA).

  • Use different passwords for different accounts
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible
  • Use a password manager to generate and store passwords
3

Think Before You Click

Take a moment to evaluate links and attachments before clicking or opening them.

  • Hover over links to preview the destination URL
  • Look for HTTPS and the padlock icon
  • Don't download attachments from unknown sources
4

Keep Software Updated

Regular updates patch security vulnerabilities that phishers might exploit.

  • Enable automatic updates for your operating system
  • Keep browsers and security software up to date
  • Update mobile apps regularly
5

Use Security Tools

Leverage technology to add extra layers of protection.

  • Install and maintain antivirus software
  • Use email filters and spam blockers
  • Consider using our AntiPhish tool for suspicious messages
6

Stay Informed

Keep up with the latest phishing tactics and security threats.

  • Follow security news and updates
  • Participate in security awareness training
  • Learn from reported phishing attempts
